### Update

Anyways, onto the update on the Z.  I had a few friends over last Monday, including the one handling the rebuild on the engine (we'll call him J going forward).  We tore the engine down in prep for it to go to the machine shop and discovered a bevy of issues that need to be addressed.  Initially J anticpiated that the head would have cracks between the cylinders as this is a pretty common issue on N47 heads such as mine.  However, upon inspection we found this not to be the case.  We discovered a few other issues:

- Lots of end play on the crank, somewhat common for manual cars
- 2 rod bearings were on the verge of spinning
- 2 rod cap studs fell out of the rods as soon as we pulled the nuts
- One main cap had some pretty good damage that transfered to the crank
- A few cylinders are mushroomed at the top
- Cylinder 1 had a broken ring
- The engine smelled horrendous when we opened it and clearly was not well maintained by previous owners

Good news is all these issues are fairly addressable.  The N47 head is known to be problematic long term, and to avoid rebuilding this engine then having the head crack on my prematurely we're opting to move to a different head.  I was able to order a P79 used from eBay to replace the N47.  This leads to us needing to move from scooped top pistons to flat tops, which poses a new issue: flat top OE style pistons have not been in stock most places for 5+ years.  I'll likely have to go with forged pistons to get overbored ones that are compatible with the P79 head.  On top of all this we're going to get a slightly hotter regrind cam to finish off a mild OEM+ performance package for this engine.

To address the rest of the issues quickly:

- The crank is barely within spec and J advised if it was his car he'd send it as is since cranks are near unobtainium for these cars.  We'll be having the damage to one bearing surface polished by the machine shop
- We're going to have the machine shop check the rods to make sure there aren't any issues with them from the studs falling out of them
- We're going to overbore to solve the mushrooming issues.  Suspect .20 over will be enough but we're pending feedback from the machine shop on this
- We're going to rebuild the N47 head so I can try to sell it to make back some of the money on buying the P79 head

All these things somewhat dispell the thought that the odometer has not rolled yet on the car.

### What's next

J took the parts to the machine shop today and we'll be pending feedback from them over the next week or two to find out what I'll need to order.  I'm holding off on buying anything till we hear from them but am currently eyeing Ross Forged pistons at .40 over.  

I have a busy couple weeks ahead of me with multipe trips to Austin both for car club events and work conferences.  I'm hoping to be able to knock out cleaning up the Transmission and start to strip down some of the equipment in the engine bay to begin the work on rust treating and painting.  

J doesn't think the machine shop and rebuild will take terribly long though I'm not sure what the exact timeline will be on things yet due to the unknown nature of things.  After Texas Z Fest I'm getting pretty antsy to keep moving on things.  My motivation on this project is higher than ever, especially being surrounded by friends pushing me to keep going.
